Gollwitzer, P. M. (1999).
Implementation intentions: Strong effects of simple plans.
American Psychologist, 54(7), 493-503.
DOI: 10.1037/0003-066X.54.7.493

Also:
Gollwitzer, P. M., & Sheeran, P. (2006).
Implementation intentions and goal achievement: A meta-analysis of effects and processes.
Advances in Experimental Social Psychology, 38, 69-119.

KEY FINDINGS:
- Implementation intentions = if-then plans: "if situation Y arises, then I will do Z"
- Meta-analysis of 94 tests: medium-to-large effect on goal attainment (d = 0.65)
- Mechanism: heightened perceptual readiness for specified cue + strong associative
  link between situation and planned action
- Essentially creates proto-habits through a single cognitive act
- Effective for: initiating goal striving, shielding ongoing pursuit from unwanted
  influences, disengaging from failing actions, conserving capability
- Intention-behavior gap: medium-to-large change in commitment (d = 0.66) only led
  to small-to-medium change in behavior (d = 0.33)
- Implementation intentions help bridge this gap
