Christakis, N. A., & Fowler, J. H. (2007).
The spread of obesity in a large social network over 32 years.
New England Journal of Medicine, 357(4), 370-379.
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMsa066082
PMID: 17652652

KEY FINDINGS:
- Framingham Heart Study network: 12,067 people, 32 years
- Friend becoming obese: +57% risk for the individual
- Mutual friend becoming obese: +171% risk
- Among siblings: +40% risk
- Among spouses: +37% risk
- Influence extended to three degrees of separation
- Similar effects found for smoking cessation and happiness

METHODOLOGICAL NOTES:
- Critiqued by Cohen-Cole & Fletcher (2008) for potential confounding
  by shared environments and homophily
- Social contagion effect likely real but magnitude may be overstated
- Statistical methods for separating contagion from homophily remain debated
