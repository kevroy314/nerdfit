Accountability: A missing construct in models of adherence behavior and in

Patient Preference and Adherence, 11, 1285-1294.

Also:
Mohr, D. C., Cuijpers, P., & Lehman, K. (2011).
Supportive accountability: A model for providing human support to enhance

Journal of Medical Internet Research, 13(1), e30.
DOI: 10.2196/jmir.1602

KEY FINDINGS:
- Accountability = expectation of account-giving involving anticipated social interaction
- Distinct from reminders or cues to action
- Existing health behavior models (HBM, SCT, TPB) fail to incorporate accountability
- Two types:
  1. Controlled Accountability: motivated by shame/duress, less effective long-term
  2. Autonomous Accountability: driven by desire to please respected providers, superior
     sustained adherence
- Internet-based weekly reporting increased acne treatment adherence by >100%
- Accountability operates through behavioral amplification independent of direct contact
- Proposed integration into Bandura's SCT, informed by SDT (competence, autonomy, relatedness)
