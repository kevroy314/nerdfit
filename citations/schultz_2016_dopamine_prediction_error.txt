Schultz, W. (2016).
Dopamine reward prediction error coding.
Dialogues in Clinical Neuroscience, 18(1), 23-32.
DOI: 10.31887/DCNS.2016.18.1/wschultz
PMID: 27069377

Key finding: Dopamine neurons encode prediction error (actual minus expected reward),
not absolute reward magnitude. Fully predicted rewards generate no response. This
grounds the reward term in the model as gamma * B * (1-H^2) -- reward signal
diminishes as behavior becomes habitual and predictable.
