Ekkekakis, P. (2009).
The dual-mode theory of affective responses to exercise in metatheoretical context.
International Review of Sport and Exercise Psychology, 2(1), 73-94 and 2(2), 119-150.
DOI (Part I): 10.1080/17509840802705920
DOI (Part II): 10.1080/17509840902829323

Key finding: Below ventilatory threshold, exercise affect is positive (cognitive
factors dominate). Above VT, affect becomes consistently negative (interoceptive
factors dominate). A 1-unit improvement in in-task feeling predicts 38 min/week
more exercise at 6 months. Not yet implemented in the model (would need an
exercise intensity parameter).
