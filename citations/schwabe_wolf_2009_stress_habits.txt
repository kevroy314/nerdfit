Schwabe, L., & Wolf, O. T. (2009).
Stress Prompts Habit Behavior in Humans.
Journal of Neuroscience, 29(22), 7191-7198.
DOI: 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.0979-09.2009
PMID: 19494141
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6666491/

KEY FINDINGS:
- Acute stress shifts behavioral control from goal-directed to habitual
- Stressed subjects chose devalued actions as often as valued ones (controls avoided devalued)
- Only 28% of stressed participants could name action-outcome associations vs. 58% of controls
- Mediated by simultaneous glucocorticoid and noradrenergic activation
- Stress weakens PFC-dependent cognitive processes while strengthening striatum-dependent automatic behaviors
- Glucocorticoids or yohimbine alone did NOT induce habit action -- both needed
- Implications for compulsive behaviors and addiction vulnerability
