Pilcher, J. J., Morris, D. M., Donnelly, J., & Feigl, H. B. (2015).
Interactions between sleep habits and self-control.
Frontiers in Human Neuroscience, 9, 284.
DOI: 10.3389/fnhum.2015.00284
PMID: 26029094
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4426706/

KEY FINDINGS:
- Sleep deprivation impairs self-control through reduced PFC capacity
- Endogenous feedback loop: glucose metabolism (influenced by circadian rhythms
  and sleep) directly affects self-regulatory capacity
- Self-control failure results from both physiological resource depletion AND
  psychological factors
- When resources severely exhausted, cognitive resources like motivation cannot
  overcome the deficit
- Sleep-deprived individuals compensate by selecting easier tasks and reducing
  performance demands
- Good sleep habits serve as essential "replenishing" mechanisms for daily
  self-regulatory capacity
