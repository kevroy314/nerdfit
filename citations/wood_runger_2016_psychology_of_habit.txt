Wood, W., & Runger, D. (2016).
Psychology of habit.
Annual Review of Psychology, 67, 289-314.
DOI: 10.1146/annurev-psych-122414-033417
PMID: 26361052

KEY FINDINGS:
- Habits characterized by cognitive, motivational, and neurobiological properties
- Three interfaces between habits and deliberate goal pursuit:
  1. Habits form as people pursue goals by repeating same responses in given context
  2. Habits and deliberate goal pursuit guide actions synergistically, though habits
     are the efficient, default mode of response
  3. People infer from habit frequency that behavior must have been intended
- Two parallel brain systems: stimulus-response (habit) and goal-directed
- Breaking habits requires: weakening S-R links, avoiding triggers, goal-directed
  inhibition, or forming competing S-R associations
- Stress and addiction understood through habit framework
- Context stability is key for habit maintenance; context change disrupts habits
