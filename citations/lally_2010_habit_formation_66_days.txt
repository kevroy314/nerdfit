Lally, P., van Jaarsveld, C. H. M., Potts, H. W. W., & Wardle, J. (2010).
How are habits formed: Modelling habit formation in the real world.
European Journal of Social Psychology, 40(6), 998-1009.
DOI: 10.1002/ejsp.674

KEY FINDINGS:
- 96 volunteers chose an eating, drinking or activity behavior to carry out daily
  in the same context for 12 weeks
- Automaticity followed an asymptotic curve
- Average time to plateau of automaticity: 66 days (range: 18-254 days)
- Early repetitions led to larger automaticity increases than later repetitions
- Simple actions (drinking water) reached plateau faster than complex routines (50 sit-ups)
- Missing a single day did not significantly reduce automaticity
- Substantial individual variation in both asymptote level and time to reach it
- Provides the mathematical form for dH/dt: diminishing returns / asymptotic growth
