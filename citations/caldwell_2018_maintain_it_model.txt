Caldwell, A. E., Masters, K. S., Peters, J. C., Bryan, A. D., Grigsby, J.,
Hooker, S. A., Wyatt, H. R., & Hill, J. O. (2018).
Harnessing Centered Identity Transformation to Reduce Executive Function Burden
for Maintenance of Health Behavior Change: The Maintain IT Model.
Health Psychology Review, 12(1), 35-69.
DOI: 10.1080/17437199.2018.1437551
PMID: 29402182
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6124500/

KEY CLAIMS:
- Sustainable behavior change requires reducing reliance on effortful executive function
- Successful maintenance involves integrating health behaviors into identity
- "Centered identity" encompasses five dimensions:
  1. Behavior-based identity (identifying as someone who performs the behavior)
  2. Role and group-based identities (family, social affiliations)
  3. Personal values alignment
  4. Perceptual lens (attributional style and implicit theories about changeability)
  5. Psychological well-being (eudaimonic dimensions)
- As behavior becomes part of identity, it requires less effortful self-regulation
- Increases resilience during setbacks
- Recommends helping individuals examine fundamental identity components and
  align behaviors with core values
