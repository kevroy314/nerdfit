THREE CORE PRINCIPLES OF IBM THEORY:
1. Action-Readiness: Identities activate preparedness to behave and interpret
   situations through the lens of norms, values, and behaviors relevant to the identity.
2. Dynamic Construction: Which identities become salient, their meaning, and
   associated behaviors are actively constructed by features of immediate contexts
   despite feeling stable.
3. Interpretation of Difficulty: When behavior feels identity-aligned, encountered
   obstacles are understood as indicating importance; when misaligned, difficulty
   suggests the behavior is pointless and "not for people like me."

KEY IMPLICATION FOR MODEL:
The same expectation gap (E) could be motivating or demoralizing depending on
identity congruence. This is a qualitative shift requiring an identity variable.
