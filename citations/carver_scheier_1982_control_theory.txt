KEY CLAIMS:
- Behavior regulated by negative feedback loops: input perception, reference value (goal),
  comparison function, output behavior
- TWO types of loops: (1) behavior-goal discrepancy (direction), (2) rate of progress
  toward discrepancy reduction (velocity)
- Velocity meta-loop generates affect: positive affect when progress exceeds expectations,
  negative when it falls short
- Meta-loop governs goal engagement vs. disengagement
- Hierarchical control: abstract goals disaggregate into concrete subordinate objectives
- Confidence/doubt about progress triggers effort changes or goal disengagement
- Sources of dysfunction: misperceiving current state, unrealistic goals, calibration
  extremes (perfectionism vs. laxness), lacking effective action knowledge
