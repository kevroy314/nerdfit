Heatherton, T. F., & Wagner, D. D. (2011).
Cognitive Neuroscience of Self-Regulation Failure.
Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 15(3), 132-139.
DOI: 10.1016/j.tics.2010.12.005
PMID: 21273114
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3062191/

KEY FINDINGS:
- Successful self-regulation depends on top-down control from PFC over subcortical
  regions (reward, emotion)
- Self-regulatory failure occurs when balance tips toward subcortical activation:
  (a) exceptionally strong impulses, or (b) compromised PFC function
- Negative emotional states decrease PFC activity while increasing ventral striatal activation
- Effortful self-control depletes circulating blood glucose, temporarily impairing PFC function
- Despite varying subcortical targets (nucleus accumbens for food/drugs, amygdala for emotions),
  lateral PFC consistently mediates successful regulation across domains
- Domain-general process: self-regulation capacity is a shared resource
