Kruglanski, A. W., & Szumowska, E. (2020).
Habitual behavior is goal-driven.
Perspectives on Psychological Science, 15(5), 1256-1271.
DOI: 10.1177/1745691620917676
PMID: 32569529

Also:
Kruglanski, A. W., et al. (2002).
A theory of goal systems.
Advances in Experimental Social Psychology, 34, 331-378.
DOI: 10.1016/S0065-2601(02)80008-9

KEY CLAIMS:
- Habitual behavior is sensitive to changes in goal properties (reward value, expectancy)
- Adjustment may be slower for habitual than non-habitual behavior due to
  routinized/automatic nature (reduced attention to consequences)
- Prolonged persistence of habits despite detachment from original goal likely stems
  from attachment to a DIFFERENT goal
- No need to postulate purposeless behavior
- Goal systems organized as hierarchical networks with:
  - Equifinality: multiple means to one goal
  - Multifinality: one means serving multiple goals
  - Counterfinality: means that serve one goal but undermine another
- Goals and identities stored in memory in interconnected, hierarchical structure
