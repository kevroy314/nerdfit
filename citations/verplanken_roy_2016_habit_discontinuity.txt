Verplanken, B., & Roy, D. (2016).
Empowering interventions to promote sustainable lifestyles: Testing the habit
discontinuity hypothesis in a field experiment.
Journal of Environmental Psychology, 45, 127-134.
DOI: 10.1016/j.jenvp.2015.11.008

Also:
Verplanken, B., Walker, I., Davis, A., & Jurasek, M. (2008).
Context change and travel mode choice: Combining the habit discontinuity and
self-activation hypotheses.
Journal of Environmental Psychology, 28(2), 121-127.

KEY FINDINGS:
- Behavior change interventions more effective during life transitions
- Intervention was more effective among recently relocated participants
- Window of opportunity: ~3 months after relocation
- Field experiment: 800 households, intervention vs. control, movers vs. non-movers
- 25 environment-related behaviors measured at baseline and 8 weeks later
- Mechanisms: unfreezing of old habits, information acquisition, value activation
- Life transitions that create windows: moving house, changing jobs, retirement,
  starting a family, school transitions
